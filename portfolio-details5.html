Lyft Uber Price Prediction

Optimizing Ride-Sharing Pricing with Predictive Analytics

The aim of this project is to understand and optimize pricing strategies for ride-sharing services such as Uber and Lyft by identifying key factors affecting price variations and demand patterns. By leveraging data science techniques, we aim to uncover temporal, geographic, and weather-related demand patterns and develop predictive models to enhance resource allocation and pricing.

Methodology

  • Data Collection and Cleaning: Datasets included historical ride data with variables such as distance, timestamp, weather conditions (pressure, temperature, wind), surge multiplier, and ride type. Performed data cleaning to handle missing values and normalize numerical features.
  • Exploratory Data Analysis (EDA): Generated heatmaps to explore correlations between features. Identified significant variables such as distance, time of day, and weather conditions (source/destination pressure and temperature) affecting ride prices.
  • Feature Engineering: Created new features, such as hour_of_day, is_weekend, and weather_categories (e.g., high pressure, wind).
  • Model Development: Built regression models (Linear Regression, Ridge, Lasso, Decision Tree, Random Forest, Neural Networks, and XGBoost) to predict ride prices and evaluated using R-squared, MAE, and RMSE scores.
  • Insights Derived: Distance is a primary factor affecting ride prices. Surge pricing increases significantly during late evenings. Lyft rides for shorter distances are cheaper, while Uber offers competitive long-distance pricing.

Visualizations

Key graphs include correlation heatmaps, line charts showing average ride price variations with distance, time, and weather, as well as bar charts comparing surge multipliers.

  • Neural Networks and XGBoost also performed well but required hyperparameter tuning.
  • Linear and Lasso regressions showed lower accuracy due to linear assumptions.

Recommendations and Business Implications

  1. Implement dynamic pricing that adjusts based on predicted demand surges.
  2. Increase driver availability in high-demand zones during peak times.
  3. Provide promotional discounts during non-peak hours to balance demand.
  4. Monitor and prepare for increased demand during local events.

Next Steps

  • Integrate real-time traffic and event data to improve prediction accuracy.
  • Deploy the Random Forest model in a Streamlit application for interactive fare predictions.
  • Continuously monitor model performance and retrain with new data.
